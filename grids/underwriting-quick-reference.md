# Underwriting Quick Reference — Agent Cheat Sheet

> The most common scenarios agents face on the phone. Use this to rapidly identify options.

---

## Tier Definitions

| Tier | Benefit | When It Pays | Best For |
|------|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Level / Preferred** | 100% face amount from day 1 | Immediately | Healthy or moderately healthy clients |
| **Standard** | 100% face amount from day 1 | Immediately | Moderate health conditions |
| **Graded** | Yr 1: 25–40% / Yr 2: 50–75% / Yr 3+: 100% | After 2-year wait | Significant health history |
| **Modified / Basic** | Yr 1: 110% premiums / Yr 2: 220% premiums / Yr 3+: 100% | After 2-year wait | High-risk health conditions |
| **Return of Premium (ROP)** | Yr 1–2: all premiums returned / Yr 3+: 100% | After 2-year wait | Alternative to graded |
| **Guaranteed Issue (GI)** | 2-year graded (premiums + interest if death in yr 1–2) | After 2-year wait | Anyone 50–85, no health questions |

---

## The "Last Resort Stack" — What Agents Do

1. **Try Level/Preferred** with best-match carrier for their conditions
2. **If declined or graded** → try another carrier with more lenient Level underwriting
3. **If all Level declined** → move to Graded with a lenient carrier (Mutual of Omaha, Foresters, Senior Choice)
4. **Last resort** → Guaranteed Issue (Aetna/Accendo, Corebridge GIWL, Gerber Life)

---

## Instant Knockouts — These End the Call for Most Carriers

| Condition | What It Means | Options |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| Currently in hospice | Terminal, near end of life | Only GI if death not expected within 12–24 months (Accendo allows GI) |
| Terminal illness (MD-certified death within 12 mo) | High imminent risk | Most: DECLINE. Accendo: DECLINE if <2yr prognosis |
| Currently incarcerated | Cannot place policy on incarcerated person | All: DECLINE. Wait until released. |
| Currently on dialysis | Kidney failure | Accendo: GI. Most others: DECLINE |
| Alzheimer's / Dementia | Cognitive impairment | Accendo: GI only. All others: DECLINE |
| ALS (Lou Gehrig's) | Progressive terminal | Accendo: GI only. All others: DECLINE |

**Key Insight**: Aetna/Accendo is often the ONLY option for the sickest prospects (ALS, dementia, dialysis, organ transplant). They accept virtually everyone via their Guaranteed Issue tier. The trade-off: GI = 2-year waiting period and higher premiums.

## Common Conditions — Fastest Carrier Options

### Heart Attack
- **<1 year ago**: Accendo GI, Americo GI → Graded after 1 year
- **1–2 years ago**: Most carriers → Graded tier
- **>2 years ago**: Most carriers → Level/Standard

### Stroke / TIA
- **<1 year ago**: Accendo GI, Americo GI → Graded after 1 year
- **TIA only (mini-stroke)**: Most carriers don't ask / allow at Level
- **>2 years ago**: Most carriers → Level/Standard

### Cancer (Non-Skin)
- **Active / current**: All → DECLINE (except Accendo if not metastatic)
- **<2 years in remission**: Accendo GI, most others Graded or DECLINE
- **>2 years in remission**: Accendo Level, Mutual Omaha Graded, most Level
- **>4 years in remission**: Most carriers → Level/Preferred
- **Basal/Squamous cell skin cancer**: Not counted — all carriers allow Level

### COPD / Emphysema
- **With oxygen**: Accendo GI, most → DECLINE
- **Without oxygen**: Accendo GI, Mutual of Omaha Graded, Americo may allow Level
- **Mild/controlled**: Many carriers allow Level

### Diabetes (Type 2)
- **Controlled, no complications**: ALL carriers → Level (diabetes alone doesn't knock anyone out)
- **Insulin before age 50**: Mutual of Omaha → Graded; others often Level
- **Complications (neuropathy, retinopathy)**: Varies — most → Graded
- **Diabetic amputation**: Accendo GI, most → DECLINE

### Kidney Disease / Failure
- **Dialysis**: Accendo GI, all others DECLINE
- **Kidney disease (not on dialysis)**: Accendo GI, Senior Choice Graded, most vary

### COPD + Heart + Diabetes (multiple conditions)
- Multiple serious conditions compound. Always check the MOST SEVERE condition first.
- Accendo/Aetna GI is almost always available as the backstop.

## Build Chart Quick Reference

Most FE carriers are VERY lenient on height/weight (BMI up to ~45–50). 
This is rarely a knockout factor for FE. Confirm with carrier if client is extremely overweight.

Americo Eagle Select build chart (from PDF):
- 5'0" → 90–227 lbs
- 5'5" → 109–275 lbs
- 5'10" → 122–310 lbs
- 6'0" → 130–328 lbs
- 6'4" → 144–365 lbs

## State-Specific Notes

- **New York**: Most FE carriers do NOT write business in NY. Accendo/Aetna, NY Life AARP are common NY options.
- **California**: Some carriers have state-specific forms and rates. Ethos works well in CA.
- **Montana, Maine, Vermont**: Smaller state restrictions — verify carrier availability.
- **Washington state**: Some carriers have restrictions on certain plans.

Always verify state availability before quoting. The FE grid shows state-by-state availability for Americo.

## Questions That Reveal the Right Tier Faster

These are the HIGH-YIELD questions — ask these first before going through the full list:

1. **"Are you currently in a nursing home, assisted living, or hospice?"** → If yes, major restriction
2. **"Are you currently on oxygen?"** → If yes, kills most Level options
3. **"Have you been diagnosed with Alzheimer's, dementia, or ALS?"** → If yes, Accendo GI only
4. **"Are you currently on dialysis?"** → If yes, Accendo GI only
5. **"Have you had cancer in the last 2 years (other than skin cancer)?"** → Major filter
6. **"Have you had a heart attack or stroke in the last year?"** → Determines if GI or graded

If all 6 are No → client likely qualifies for Level/Standard with multiple carriers.
If any are Yes → immediately narrow to appropriate carriers.
