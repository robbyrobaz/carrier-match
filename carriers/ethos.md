## Key Underwriting Concepts

### "Ineligible" and Routing
**Important**: In Ethos guidelines, "Ineligible" often means the applicant is **routed to a different product** rather than an outright decline.

Examples:
- High-risk term applicant → Ethos Select
- Moderate-risk → Spectrum
- Best risk → Prime
- Some conditions → Ethos Final Expense instead of term

### Chronic Condition Management
- **Well-controlled chronic conditions**: Often acceptable at Prime level
- **Recent diagnosis/treatment**: May route to Spectrum or Select
- **Multiple comorbidities**: Closer review required

## Knockout Conditions (Frequent Declines)

### Severe/Terminal Conditions
- **Active Cancer** (recent diagnosis/treatment)
- **Terminal Illness** (expected death <12 months)
- **Organ Transplant** (recent)
- **Major Neurological Disorders** (Huntington's, MS with specific treatment)
- **Dialysis/ESRD** (end-stage renal disease)
- **Dementia/Alzheimer's** (recent diagnosis)

### Recent Major Medical Events
- **Heart Attack** — typically decline if <1 year
- **Stroke** — typically decline if <1-5 years (depends on product)
- **Heart Surgery** — typically decline if <1 year
- **Multiple Amputations** — typically decline

### Behavioral/Legal
- **Incarceration** — within past 10 years typically decline/ineligible
- **Felony Conviction** — within past 10 years often decline/ineligible
- **Active Parole/Probation** — typically ineligible

### Substance Use
- **Active Drug Use** — within past 2-10 years (varies by product)
- **Alcohol Abuse** — recent treatment within past 5-10 years
- **Multiple DUI/DWI** — typically decline/ineligible

### Other
- **Suicidal Ideation/Attempts** — within past 2-5 years
- **Disability** — on SSI/SSDI for medical reasons (some products)
- **Bipolar Disorder** — varies by product; some require medication stability
- **Schizophrenia** — varies; may be routed to lower tier

## Digital Underwriting Process

### eApplication
- Online questionnaire
- Real-time decisioning on many cases
- No medical exams for simplified issue products
- Instant approval possible

### Medical Underwriting
- APS (Attending Physician Statement) may be requested
- MIB (Medical Information Bureau) check
- Pharmacy records review (RxHistory)

### Non-Medical Underwriting
- Age and income verification
- Zip code rating
- Tobacco status (online verification often used)

## Key Underwriting Decisions Tree

```
Applicant applies for Ethos Term Life

     ↓
Is applicant excellent/good health with well-controlled conditions?
     ├─ YES → PRIME tier (up to $2M, standard rates)
     │
     └─ NO → Continue
               ↓
        Is applicant good-fair health, no severe recent conditions?
             ├─ YES → SPECTRUM tier (up to $500K, moderate rates)
             │
             └─ NO → Continue
                     ↓
                 Is applicant average-fair health, not disabled?
                      ├─ YES → SELECT tier (up to $150K, higher rates)
                      │
                      └─ NO → DECLINE or Route to Final Expense/IUL
```

## Important Notes

- **Routing, Not Decline**: Ethos commonly routes applicants to lower-tier products or other carriers rather than straight declines
- **Medical Exam Waiver**: Most Ethos products simplified issue (no exam required)
- **Age 85 Cutoff**: Maximum issue age 85 for most products
- **Tobacco Premium**: Significant tobacco surcharge (+40-100% typical)
- **Multiple Comorbidities**: More underwriting scrutiny with several conditions
- **Recent Medical Events**: 1-2 year lookback typical; conditions within this window often route to lower tier
