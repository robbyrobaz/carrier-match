# Aetna / Accendo — Final Expense Products

**Carrier**: Accendo Insurance Company (CVS Health affiliate)
**Parent Company**: Aetna/CVS Health
**Product Type**: Individual Whole Life Insurance

---

## Products

### Level Preferred Plan
- **Issue Ages**: 45-85
- **Face Amounts**: $2,000 - $50,000
- **Underwriting**: Standard rates with preferred pricing for good health

### Level Standard Plan
- **Issue Ages**: 45-85
- **Face Amounts**: $2,000 - $50,000
- **Underwriting**: Standard rates

### Modified Plan
- **Issue Ages**: 45-85
- **Face Amounts**: $2,000 - $50,000
- **Death Benefit**: Modified/Graded (2-year grace period, limited first 2 years)
- **Underwriting**: For applicants with recent health events or conditions

## Key Underwriting Features

### Most Flexible Conditions
Accendo is notably flexible with "Guaranteed Issue" approval for:
- **AIDS/HIV/ARC** (Level Preferred/Standard)
- **Congestive Heart Failure**
- **Dementia/Alzheimer's**
- **Diabetic Complications** (within 2 years)
- **Cirrhosis** (Guaranteed Issue on Level Preferred)
- **Emphysema** (Guaranteed Issue)
- **COPD** (Guaranteed Issue)
- **Hepatitis B & C** (Guaranteed Issue)
- **Liver Disease** (Guaranteed Issue)
- **Organ Transplant** (Guaranteed Issue)
- **Kidney Dialysis** (Guaranteed Issue)
- **Cystic Fibrosis** (Guaranteed)

### Mid-Tier Conditions (GI or Graded)
- **Angina**: <1yr GI on Level Preferred/Standard
- **Heart Attack**: <1yr GI
- **Heart Surgery/Valve Replacement**: <1yr GI
- **Stroke/TIA**: <1yr GI; TIA "not asked - allowed"
- **Stent**: <1yr GI
- **Pacemaker/Defibrillator**: <1yr GI
- **Alcohol/Drug Abuse**: <2yrs Guaranteed Issue
- **ALS**: Guaranteed Issue
- **AFIB/Irregular Heartbeat**: "Not asked - Allowed"

### Declines
- **Mental Incapacity** — Standard decline
- **Assisted Living/Long Term Care** — <6 months Guaranteed Issue; current — DECLINE
- **Terminal Illness** — Death within 2 years — DECLINE
- **Jail/Incarcerated** — DECLINE

## Declinable Medications by Condition

### Cancer Medications (Unacceptable)
- Abacavir, Abemaciclib, Abiraterone
- Adcetris, Adriamycin, Adrucil
- Afinitor, and many other cancer therapeutics
- **Check full drug list** — new medications regularly added

### Congestive Heart Failure Medications
- ACE inhibitors (Accupril, Aceon)
- Beta-blockers and other cardiac medications
- See full drug list for specific agents

### COPD/Respiratory Medications
- Advair, Aclidinium, Airmir (inhalers)
- Respiratory maintenance medications
- Check medication list carefully

### Angina Medications
- Nitrates and cardiac anti-anginal agents

### General Guidance
- **No condition noted**: May be unacceptable for any condition prescribed
- **Multiple uses**: Must provide condition medication is prescribed for
- **Duplicate listings**: Some medications unacceptable universally; others unacceptable for specific conditions
- **Plans vary**: Level Preferred may decline medication; Level Standard or Modified may approve
- **Drug list regularly updated**: Contact underwriting if uncertain

## Application & Support

**Underwriting Contact**: For questions on medications or condition interpretation, contact Accendo underwriting department directly

**Applicant Awareness**: Applicant may not disclose declinable condition on application, but prescribed medications may indicate its existence

**Plan Flexibility**: If medication/condition unacceptable for Level Preferred, try Level Standard or Modified plans — applicant may be eligible

## Key Agent Notes

### Strongest for Applicants With:
- Serious cardiac conditions (Accendo flexible with GI)
- Complex organ system issues (cirrhosis, liver disease, dialysis)
- Recent major medical events (recent heart surgery, stent, etc.)

### Consider Alternative Carriers If:
- Applicant in assisted living (current — DECLINE)
- Terminal illness or imminent death expected
- Mental incapacity (generally better with New Vista, Senior Choice)

### Always:
- Review medication list against the complete declinable drugs list
- Verify condition-medication alignment
- Consider plan tier (Preferred vs. Standard vs. Modified) based on severity and timing
- Ask about oral vs. injectable medications — sometimes affects underwriting
