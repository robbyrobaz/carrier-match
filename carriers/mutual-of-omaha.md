## Underwriting Strengths (Key Advantages)

### Medical Flexibility
- **Asthma**: Mild asthma may be eligible for Preferred tier
- **Sleep Apnea**: Mild sleep apnea eligible for Preferred WITH verified CPAP usage
- **Cholesterol/Hypertension**: Treatment alone does NOT exclude Preferred Plus, Preferred, or Standard Plus classes
- **Unisex Build Charts**: Same height/weight standards for males and females

### Tobacco/Nicotine
- **Occasional Cigar Users**: 2 cigars/month or less can qualify for nontobacco rates with negative urinalysis
- **Marijuana Users**: Occasional use may qualify for Preferred or Standard Plus nontobacco rates
- **Preferred Tobacco Class**: Available for smokers

### Family History
- **Age 60+**: Family history qualifications do NOT apply
- **Gender-Specific Cancers**: Don't apply if opposite sex persons
- **Death vs. Disease**: Family history applies to deaths, not disease diagnosis
- **Diabetes Deaths**: Can qualify for Preferred Plus/Preferred even with family history

### Build Chart
- Unisex charts (same for males and females)
- More flexible than some competitors
- Overweight not automatic decline if health is otherwise good

## Risk Classes and Underwriting Tiers

### Preferred Plus (Best Risk)
- Excellent health
- No significant medical conditions
- Nonsmoker
- Ideal weight/build
- Coverage: Up to $2M+

### Preferred (Good Health)
- Good health, minimal conditions
- Well-controlled chronic conditions
- Nonsmoker
- Acceptable weight/build
- Coverage: Up to $1M+

### Standard Plus (Fair Health)
- Some medical conditions
- Light smoker possibly accepted
- Larger build acceptable
- Coverage: Up to $500K

### Standard (Acceptable Health)
- Multiple conditions or recent treatment
- Smoker acceptable
- Coverage: Up to $250K

## Application Types and Processes

### Fully Underwritten Applications
- Full medical underwriting
- May require medical exam (EKG, blood work, etc.)
- Attending Physician Statements (APS) for significant conditions
- Motor Vehicle Records (MVR) pulled
- Inspection reports available
- Slower process but thorough (10-15 business days typical)

### Simplified Applications
- Limited health questions
- No medical exam for lower amounts
- Faster decisioning (5-7 days typical)
- Available for Express TLE, GULE, IULE

### Whole Life Application
- Modified simplified underwriting
- Generally requires telephone interview
- Some health questions
- Faster than fully underwritten

## Conditional Receipt and TIA

### Conditional Receipt
- Available for approved cases with payment
- Coverage effective when payment received (subject to conditions)
- Policy issued subject to final underwriting approval

### Temporary Life Insurance Agreement (TIA)
- Provides temporary coverage pending final underwriting
- Effective when application and payment received
- Usually 90 days maximum duration
- Coverage amount equals proposed amount (if approved) or lower amount if rated

## Medical Underwriting Strengths

### Conditions Managed Well by Mutual of Omaha
- **Mild to Moderate Hypertension**: Not excluded, especially if controlled
- **Type 2 Diabetes**: Acceptable if well-controlled without insulin
- **Mild Asthma**: Can qualify for Preferred with good control
- **Sleep Apnea**: Preferred available with CPAP usage compliance
- **History of Anxiety/Depression**: Can be approved if treated and stable
- **Recovering Substance Use**: Can be approved if sufficient time elapsed (varies 3-10 years)

### Typical Declines
- Active cancer treatment or recent diagnosis (<1 year)
- Dialysis or end-stage renal disease
- Terminal illness diagnosis
- Severe cognitive decline (dementia in advanced stages)
- Recent stroke (<6-12 months)
- Active alcoholism or substance use

## Key Underwriting Questions

### Medical History
- Current conditions and diagnoses
- Medical treatment dates
- Surgeries, hospitalizations
- Medications and dosages
- Family history (unless age 60+)

### Lifestyle
- Tobacco/nicotine use
- Alcohol consumption (critical for some carriers)
- Occupation (hazardous jobs may require additional underwriting)
- Avocations (aviation, mountaineering, etc.)
- Driving record (MVR reviewed)

### Occupations and Avocations
- See Occupations section (pages 42-44) for specific occupational restrictions
- Aviation: Pilot restrictions apply
- Hazmat exposure: May require rating or decline
- High-risk hobbies: Requires additional underwriting

---

## Non-Smoker/Non-Nicotine Qualifications

To qualify for nonsmoker rates:
- No tobacco use in past 12 months
- **No nicotine use** in past 12 months (includes patches, gum, vaping)
- Exception: Occasional cigars (2/month) with negative urinalysis
- Exception: Marijuana use with negative urinalysis in some cases

Tobacco/nicotine within past 12 months = smoker rates (typically +50-150% depending on class)

## Important Programs and Features

### Accelerated Underwriting Program
- Available for certain applicants meeting health criteria
- Faster decision (may be same-day)
- Limited to specified age/amount combinations
- Lower exam requirements

### Statement of Policyowner Intent
- Required for large cases ($500K+)
- Demonstrates legitimate insurance need
- Prevents anti-selection/fraud

### Premium Funding Acknowledgment
- Required when third-party premium funding involved
- Verifies source of funds

### Term Conversion Guidelines
- Most term policies convertible to permanent without medical exam
- Conversion period: 10-25 years depending on product
- Available until specific age (usually 75-80)

---

## Rating and Decisions

### Preferred Plus Rating
- Standard rates (0% additional premium)
- Excellent health, nonsmoker, good build

### Preferred Rating
- 0-15% additional premium
- Good health with minor conditions

### Standard Plus Rating
- 15-50% additional premium
- Fair health, some conditions, possibly smoker

### Standard Rating
- 50-100%+ additional premium
- Multiple conditions or significant impairments
- Smoker status likely
