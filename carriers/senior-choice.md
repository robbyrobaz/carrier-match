# Senior Choice — Whole Life Insurance (Ages 50-85)

**Carrier**: Senior Choice Life Insurance Company
**Product Type**: Whole Life Insurance (Non-Participating)
**Issue Ages**: 50-85
**Document**: Agent Guide (Form CN14-001, 4/19)

---

## Plans Available

### Immediate Death Benefit Plan (Policy Form 9767)
**Death Benefit**: Immediate full benefit from day one
**Best For**: Applicants with good health, wanting immediate coverage
**Premium**: Level premiums throughout life

### Graded Death Benefit Plan (Policy Form 9644)
**Death Benefit**: Graded over first 2-3 years, then 100%
- Year 1-2: Reduced benefit (typically 30-50% of face amount)
- Year 3+: 100% of scheduled face amount
**Best For**: Applicants with recent health conditions or medical events
**Premium**: Level premiums throughout life

### Return of Premium Benefit Plan (Policy Form 9645)
**Death Benefit**: Return of all premiums paid if death occurs within first 2 years
**After Year 2**: Normal face amount
**Best For**: Applicants concerned about premium recovery
**Premium**: Slightly higher than standard due to ROP feature

## Underwriting Questions

### Standard Medical History
- Current health conditions
- Medical treatment history
- Hospital admissions
- Surgeries and procedures
- Medications currently taking

### Key Conditions Evaluated
- Diabetes (especially if insulin-dependent)
- Heart disease, angina, heart attacks
- Stroke or TIA
- Cancer (type, when diagnosed, treatment)
- Chronic respiratory conditions (COPD, asthma, emphysema)
- Cirrhosis and liver disease
- Kidney disease or dialysis
- Mental health conditions
- Alzheimer's/dementia

### Lifestyle Questions
- Tobacco/nicotine use (critical underwriting factor)
- Alcohol consumption
- Occupational hazards
- Avocational activities (aviation, hazmat, etc.)

## Underwriting Tiers

### Preferred (Best Risk)
- Excellent health
- No major medical conditions
- Well-controlled chronic conditions
- Good family history
- Nonsmoker, normal build

### Standard (Good Health)
- Good general health
- May have 1-2 mild chronic conditions
- Conditions well-managed with medication
- Nonsmoker or light smoker
- Within build limits

### Basic (Acceptable Health)
- Graded or Modified plans
- History of medical conditions
- Recent treatment or diagnosis
- Smoker acceptable
- More flexible on build

## Riders Available

### Accidental Death Benefit Rider
- Provides additional death benefit if death by accident
- Rider face amount: $5,000 - $50,000 (varies by policy)
- Premium: Additional cost

### Grandchild Rider
- Provides coverage for grandchildren
- Available for policies on applicants age 50+
- Coverage amounts: $5,000 - $25,000 per grandchild

### Nursing Home Waiver of Premium Rider
- Waives premium payments if confined to nursing home
- Covered care must be 24-hour facility care
- Effective after qualification period

### Children's Insurance Agreement
- Rider providing coverage for minor children
- Benefit amount: $5,000 - $10,000 per child
- Automatic conversion at age of majority

### Terminal Illness Rider
- Accelerated benefit if diagnosed with terminal illness
- May receive portion of death benefit before death
- Conditions and amounts specified in rider

### Confined Care Rider
- Coverage for confinement in skilled nursing facility
- Acts as supplement to base policy
- Premium varies by age and issue amount

## Key Underwriting Scenarios

### Strong Approval Cases
- Age 50-75, good health, nonsmoker
- Controlled hypertension or mild diabetes
- History of successful cancer treatment (3+ years disease-free)
- Recent cardiac event (applies for Graded plan)

### Challenging Cases
- Advanced age (78-85) with multiple conditions
- Recent cancer diagnosis (<1 year)
- Dialysis or kidney failure
- Active substance abuse
- Terminal illness diagnosis

### Better Options Than Senior Choice
- Ethos Final Expense (more liberal with severe conditions)
- Accendo (offers Guaranteed Issue for many impairments)

## Special Underwriting Notes

### Key Strengths
- Good for applicants ages 50-85
- Graded and ROP plans flexible for applicants with recent health events
- Whole life provides lifetime coverage with guaranteed level premiums
- Build chart more lenient than some competitors

### Timing Considerations
- Telephone interview turnaround: 2-5 business days
- Decision usually same day or next business day after interview
- Policy delivery: 10-15 business days after approval (subject to state approvals)

### Age Considerations
- Ages 50-70: Most favorable underwriting
- Ages 71-80: Standard requirements apply, more conditions evaluated
- Ages 81-85: More stringent, fewer medical conditions acceptable
