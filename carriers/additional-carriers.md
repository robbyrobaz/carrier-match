# Additional Carriers — Research Notes

> Data sourced from agent training materials, carrier websites, and industry knowledge.

## American Amicable (Great Plains Life Insurance)

- **Products**: Pioneer Plus Whole Life, Dignity Solutions
- **Issue Ages**: 0–85 (FE focus: 45–85)
- **Face Amounts**: $5,000–$50,000
- **Underwriting Tiers**:
  - **Level (Immediate)**: Full benefit from day 1
  - **Graded**: 2-year waiting period
  - **Return of Premium**: Premiums returned if death in years 1–2
- **Known For**: Accepts diabetics well, heart conditions, lenient on many conditions
- **Payment**: EFT from checking account only (no credit cards)
- **Chime/Online Bank**: Does NOT accept online-only banks
- **Key Strength**: Competitive rates for diabetics; accepts insulin-dependent diabetics
- **Agent Portal**: americanamicable.com
- **AM Best Rating**: A- (Excellent)

## Corebridge Financial (formerly AIG Life & Retirement)

- **Products**:
  - **Guaranteed Issue Whole Life (GIWL)**: Ages 50–80, $5K–$25K — NO health questions
  - **Value+ Protector**: Ages 18–80, simplified issue
- **Issue Ages**: 50–80 (GI), 18–80 (simplified)
- **Face Amounts**: $5,000–$25,000 (GI); higher for simplified
- **Underwriting**: GIWL = no health questions at all (automatic GI)
- **Waiting Period**: 2-year graded for GIWL (if death in years 1–2, return of premiums + 10%)
- **Payment**: EFT, debit card
- **Chime/Online Bank**: Verify with carrier (historically restricted)
- **Key Strength**: True GI with no knockout questions — works for anyone 50–80
- **Notes**: Strong brand recognition with seniors; AARP partnership products are through NY Life (separate)
- **AM Best Rating**: A (Excellent)

## Foresters Financial (PlanRight Whole Life)

- **Products**: PlanRight Whole Life (3 tiers)
- **Issue Ages**: 50–85
- **Face Amounts**: $2,000–$35,000
- **Underwriting Tiers**:
  - **Preferred**: Full immediate benefit. Stricter health requirements.
  - **Standard**: Full immediate benefit. Moderate health conditions OK.
  - **Basic**: Graded benefit (year 1: 30% of face, year 2: 70%, year 3+: 100%)
- **Key Conditions**:
  - COPD: Standard tier
  - Diabetes: Preferred tier (type 2, controlled)
  - Heart attack >2 yrs: Standard
  - Cancer >2 yrs (non-metastatic): Standard
  - CHF: Basic tier
  - Alzheimer's/Dementia: DECLINE
  - Currently on oxygen: DECLINE
- **Payment**: EFT, debit, or credit card accepted
- **Chime/Online Bank**: Does NOT accept (requires traditional bank)
- **Unique Benefit**: Fraternal benefits — emergency assistance funds, scholarship programs for members' families, community grants. No other FE carrier offers this.
- **Key Strength**: Accepts many conditions that competitors decline; competitive rates; strong brand
- **AM Best Rating**: A (Excellent)
- **Notes**: Foresters is a fraternal benefit society, not just an insurance company. Members get additional non-insurance benefits.

## National Life Group (Life Insurance Company of the Southwest / LSW)

- **Products**: LifeQuote FE, Life Protection Advantage IUL
- **Issue Ages**: 0–80 (FE)
- **Face Amounts**: $5,000–$50,000
- **Underwriting**: Simplified issue (health questions, no exam)
- **Key Strength**: Good for diabetics; competitive IUL products for agents offering index products
- **Payment**: EFT from checking
- **Chime/Online Bank**: Does NOT accept online-only banks
- **AM Best Rating**: A+ (Superior)
- **Notes**: Strong in the IUL space; FE product is secondary to their main business. Better known for wealth-building products.

## ⚠️ Note on "Instabrain"

Instabrain is **NOT an insurance carrier** — it is an agent productivity tool (CRM, dialer, lead management platform for FE agents). It should not appear in the carrier matching results. If an agent is using Instabrain, it's the software they use to manage leads and calls, not a carrier they place business with.

> **Direct Express**: Many SSI/SSDI recipients receive payments on a Direct Express Mastercard debit card. This is different from Chime. Most carriers accept it as a debit card.

> **Chime/Varo/Current/Revolut**: Online-only fintechs. Many traditional carriers' systems cannot process these. When in doubt, ask the prospect to check if they have a routing number AND account number (online banks have these but some carriers' systems still reject them).
